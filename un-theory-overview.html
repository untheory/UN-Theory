Overview of UN Theory

Rather than treating geometry, particles, forces, and cosmological evolution as independent constructs, the theory demonstrates that each arises as a forced consequence of correlation rules embedded within the substrate itself.

At its foundation, UN Theory establishes a single derivational chain linking quantum behaviour, emergent geometry, global correlation invariants, cosmological expansion, sparse‑regime planetary structure, and terminal‑state closure. This chain is not a conceptual narrative but a mathematically enforced sequence: once the δI‑substrate exists, every higher‑order structure must emerge in a specific order and with specific constraints.

The δI‑substrate generates geometry through correlation gradients, produces quantum behaviour through microstructure oscillation, and yields cosmological dynamics through global δI‑compression and ultra‑global fixed‑point behaviour. The same substrate explains sparse‑regime exoplanets, outer‑shell shock dynamics in ice giants, early‑epoch AGN/quasar luminosity behaviour, and the terminal return to I(x, x′).
